Slow phone charging problem:

Please find out if the problem is from home or from the phone itself:
The method is very simple using the program ACCUBATTERY Taking into account other factors such as:
1. Phone temperature is high:
The high temperature of the phone causes the charging process from the thermal controller to slow down to keep the phone condition at a safe level
The temperature of the phone is supposed to be between 30-35 °C more than that. It is considered high, and the increase in temperature causes the decomposition of the chemical reactants in the battery, its capacity and capacity decrease, and the worst case of its explosion
2. Bad radio signal:
This causes the weak radio signal to increase the power from the phone to search for a good signal, and this increases the temperature of the phone
The radio wave signal is measured in decibels dBm and you can test it by yourself from
About Phone >> Status information >> SIM Card Status >> Signal Strength
You will see a number between -10 to -110. The higher the number, the weaker the signal, and the radio tower has a problem with a weak battery, or the tower is far from you The high number indicates a good radio signal, so it is preferable to put the phone in flight mode while charging
3. Extensive CPU usage:
If there are programs that run in the background on their own, you need to put these applications in the Sleeping apps list or make them force stop
Active background services option is only known from developer options
Developer Option >> Running Services
4. Phone and charger specifications:
If it does not write 9V on the charger, it is not considered a fast charger
5V chargers have a maximum limit of 1.2A and cannot give you a current of 2A or more
And you cannot charge from a fast charger if the phone connection is USB Type C
- How to check the speed of the charging current: using a program ACCU Battery
The phone is connected to the electrical socket and the readings are monitored on the ACCU program after a minute because the results are not instant and it takes time to calculate the current value and display it
Normal charging speed for chargers 5V Electric Current: 700 ma ~ 1200 ma
Normal charging speed for 9V chargers after activating the fast charging option:
Electric Current: 1300 ~ 2200ma + Depends on the company
Knowing that when the temperature rises, the charging current decreases due to excessive heat
And the battery capacity increases, fast charging cancels and switches to normal charging quickly 1A
Note: Fast charging is activated when the battery capacity is from 0~75% of the battery capacity and the value may vary according to the standards of the phone manufacturer and after the capacity
Fast charging switches to normal charging and the charging current decreases If you are within the standards and the charging current is still slow, please change the location of the electrical plug and read the results again through ACCU

Fast discharging current and fast battery discharging

" A discharge current above 500mA is considered excessive drain and the battery drains at an undesirable speed
The reasons that lead to an increase in the discharge current to a value of 1.2A, which is a high number to reduce it, we follow these steps:
1. Stop background processes:
2. Disable Bloatware applications such as Google, Gmail and any program that is allowed to be disabled Except for two programs
• Google play services and Android system WebView
3. Put all applications in the list of Deep sleeping apps and not within Sleeping apps because they are two different options
Settings >> Battery >> Background Usage Limits >> Deep Sleeping Apps
4. And activate the option in the same menu:
Settings >> Battery >> Background Usage Limits >> Put Unused Apps to sleep
5. Enable the power saving option and limit the CPU speed to 70% and the others are activated from the same menu:
Settings >> Battery >> Power saving mode
6. Also enable:
Settings >> Battery >> More Battery Settings >> adaptive Battery
7. Uninstall unused applications and delete cache of other programs
8. RAM Monitor the number of processes running in the background by activating the developer options:
Developer Option >> Running Services
9. Option control Standby apps:
Developer Option >> Standby App
Active: The apps that you use the most often, on a daily basis. These are the apps that will have access to maximum battery life and CPU memory. They are allowed to function fully according to their default settings.
Working Set: These are the apps that are not used as frequently, but which are still used regularly enough. Medium-level restrictions are placed on such apps, restricting their ability to run jobs or trigger alarms
Frequent: The apps that are used infrequently. Along with greater restrictions, a cap is imposed on high-priority FCM services.
Rare: These are the kind of apps that people put in their phone and then forget about for long periods and have heavy restrictions placed on them. A limit is placed on such apps’ ability to connect to the Internet, along with severely restricting their ability to run jobs, trigger alarms and receive high-priority FCM messages.
Never: The apps that you never use but which you never got around to uninstalling from your phone. You may not use these apps very often, but they are taking up memory space on your phone and consuming battery life while they operate unseen in the background. These are subject to the most severe restrictions to stop them from needlessly sucking up your phone’s battery.
Note that some phone models carry a “Never” category for standby mode, while others only carry the previous four.
10. Set window animation:
Developer Option > Window Animation Scale > X0.5 11. Turn off location, sync, and bluetooth services when not in use
12. The presence of bad coverage causes the phone to increase energy as a result of the continuous search for better radio broadcast quality
The Screen:
1. Reduce the brightness to 50% or 30%
In the case of the Super AMOLED screen, we also reduce the brightness to a level below the average 30% and use the option of dark mode and use black backgrounds
Feature:
The pixel is the one that emits light. In the case of black, when the screen is black, the pixels are turned off, therefore, the screen is off and does not consume power. With an AMOLED panel, it makes sense to use a back
2. Reduce Animations and Remove Widgets from the Home Screen
3. Lower the screen frequency if your phone supports this feature
4. Do not use live and animated backgrounds or video "
